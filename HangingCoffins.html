"The Hanging Coffins of Sagada hold deep spiritual meaning for the Kankanaey people, who believe that placing the dead higher brings them closer to the heavens and their ancestors. This practice also helped preserve bodies better than traditional burials and protected them from headhunters and other threats. In this article, I'll explore the history and purpose behind this unique burial tradition."
